About the Lab

Bridging Learning & Decision-Making

The Adaptive Decision Intelligence Lab (ADIL) at Carnegie Mellon University investigates the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and sequential decision-making under uncertainty.

Our work spans theoretical foundations and real-world applications — from energy systems resilience to AI-assisted planning — with an emphasis on methods that are both statistically rigorous and practically deployable.

What We Do

Research Areas

🎯

Decision-Focused Learning

Integrating predictive models directly into optimization pipelines so that learned representations are calibrated for downstream decision quality, not just prediction accuracy.

🛡

Robust Optimization

Developing optimization frameworks that remain reliable under distributional shift, model misspecification, and adversarial perturbations.

⚡

Energy Systems Resilience

Applying data-driven methods to power grid planning, demand forecasting, and infrastructure resilience under extreme events and climate uncertainty.

🤖

AI for High-Stakes Decisions

Designing trustworthy AI systems for critical domains — healthcare, logistics, and public safety — where errors carry significant real-world consequences.
