What We Do

Research

We build principled methods at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and real-world decision-making.

🎯

Decision-Focused Learning

Traditional ML pipelines separate prediction from optimization. We integrate learned models directly into optimization loops, training representations that minimize downstream decision regret rather than prediction error alone.

Our methods address challenges including non-differentiable optimization layers, combinatorial action spaces, and distribution shift between train and deploy environments.

Predict-then-Optimize Task-Based Learning End-to-End Differentiable Optimization
🛡

Robust Optimization & Uncertainty

Real decisions are made under uncertainty. We develop optimization frameworks that remain reliable under distributional shift, model misspecification, and adversarial perturbations — without being overly conservative.

This includes distributionally robust optimization, conformal prediction for optimization, and data-driven ambiguity set construction.

Distributionally Robust Optimization Uncertainty Quantification Conformal Prediction
⚡

Energy Systems Resilience

Power grids face growing uncertainty from renewable variability, extreme weather, and evolving demand patterns. We apply data-driven optimization and machine learning to grid planning, dispatch, and resilience under stress.

Application areas include electricity market design, demand forecasting, infrastructure hardening, and post-disaster restoration planning.

Power Grid Optimization Demand Forecasting Infrastructure Resilience Electricity Markets
🤖

AI for High-Stakes Decisions

Deploying AI in healthcare, logistics, and public safety requires more than accuracy — it requires reliability, interpretability, and accountability. We design trustworthy AI systems that support human experts in consequential decisions.

Trustworthy AI Human-in-the-Loop Safety-Critical Systems
📈

Online Learning & Sequential Decisions

Many real-world problems unfold over time — prices shift, resources deplete, and the environment responds to our choices. We study online learning, multi-armed bandits, and reinforcement learning for such sequential settings, with emphasis on regret minimization and safe exploration.

Online Learning Bandits Reinforcement Learning Regret Minimization
